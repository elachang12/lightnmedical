Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Lightn Medical, our services, appointments, insurance, and more. If you don't see your question answered here, please don't hesitate to contact us directly.

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans including Blue Cross Blue Shield (excluding "My Blue Health" plans), UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna, and Medicare. Please contact our office to verify your specific coverage. We're committed to making healthcare accessible and will work with your insurance to maximize your benefits.

Do you accept new patients?

Yes! We are actively accepting new patients 18 years and older. We welcome individuals looking for comprehensive primary care services in the Austin area. You can schedule your first appointment online or by calling our office directly.

What is your cancellation policy?

We request that you cancel or reschedule appointments at least 24 hours in advance whenever possible. This allows us to offer the time slot to other patients in need of care. Patients who arrive more than 15 minutes late may be asked to reschedule to ensure we can provide proper care to all our patients.

What services are included in a wellness exam?

Most insurance plans cover one annual wellness exam per year at no cost to you. This typically includes a comprehensive health review, preventive screenings, and age-appropriate health assessments. However, addressing new health concerns or managing chronic conditions during the same visit may involve additional charges. Our team can help you understand what your specific insurance covers.

How do I get prescription refills?

For prescription refills, please contact your pharmacy directly and they will send us a refill request. For controlled substances, you may need to schedule an appointment. We recommend requesting refills at least one week before you run out of medication to allow for processing time.

Do you offer telemedicine appointments?

Yes, we offer telemedicine visits for many types of appointments through our secure, HIPAA-compliant platform. This is convenient for follow-up visits, medication management, and certain types of consultations. Schedule a telemedicine appointment just like you would an in-person visit.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring your insurance card, photo ID, a list of current medications (including dosages), and any relevant medical records from previous providers. If you've completed new patient forms online, you don't need to bring printed copies. Arriving 15 minutes early will help ensure we have all necessary information.

Still have questions?

Our team is here to help. Contact us directly for personalized assistance with any questions about our services, insurance, or appointments.
